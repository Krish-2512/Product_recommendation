\section{Project in One Paragraph}

This project builds an automated product recommendation engine for ICICI Bank. 20{,}000 synthetic customers are generated using RBI statistics, then 160 features are engineered per customer, four ML models are trained (XGBoost + CatBoost ensemble, Decision Tree, AdaBoost, Logistic Regression), and a utility-based ranking decides which of 9 financial products to recommend — balancing customer fit, bank profit, and bank risk. Everything is exposed through a 4-tab Streamlit web app with an AI chatbot, new customer prediction, and a full pipeline transparency view.

\subsection{Training Steps}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item Merge all 7 CSVs on \texttt{customer\_id} → 20{,}000 row master table
  \item Engineer all 160 features (Sections 3A–3M)
  \item Remove target-leaking columns; filter to 9 valid products
  \item 80/20 stratified split (stratified on recommended product label)
  \item SMOTE oversampling for minority classes ($<$500 samples)
  \item Optuna hyperparameter search (5 trials) → best XGBoost params
  \item Train XGBoost → train CatBoost → ensemble: 0.55$\times$XGB + 0.45$\times$CB
  \item \textbf{Isotonic calibration} on held-out set (reduces overconfidence in probabilities)
  \item SHAP TreeExplainer on XGBoost → \texttt{shap\_top\_features.csv} (top-3 features per customer)
  \item Save: \texttt{xgb\_model.pkl}, \texttt{catboost\_model.pkl}, \texttt{label\_encoder.pkl}, \texttt{feature\_cols.pkl}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Utility Formula (Final Ranking Score)}

Raw ML probability is not used directly. A utility score balances three objectives:

\[
\text{Utility}(p) = \Bigl(
  \underbrace{P_{\text{ML}}(p) \times 0.45}_{\text{customer fit}} +
  \underbrace{\tfrac{\text{Profit}(p)}{100} \times 0.35}_{\text{bank profit}} -
  \underbrace{\tfrac{\text{BankRisk}(p)}{100} \times 0.20}_{\text{bank risk}}
\Bigr) \times S(p)
\]

$S(p)$ = saturation multiplier: education\_loan=1.10, credit\_card=1.10, insurance=1.05, car/consumer=1.05, home/business=1.00, personal=0.95, gold=0.75.

\textbf{Bank Risk Score per product:}
\[
\text{BankRisk}(p) = \text{clip}\bigl(\text{base}(p) + \Delta_\text{CIBIL} + \Delta_\text{repay} + \Delta_\text{FOIR} + \Delta_\text{DTI} + \Delta_\text{bureau},\; 0,\; 100\bigr)
\]

\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Product Bases} & & \\
\midrule
personal=38 & business=42 & credit\_card=28 \\
consumer=30 & education=25 & home=20 \\
car=22 & gold=12 & insurance=5 \\
\midrule
\textbf{Adjustment} & \textbf{Condition} & \textbf{Value} \\
\midrule
CIBIL & excellent/good/risky/high\_risk & $-18$/$-8$/+15/+30 \\
Repayment & regular/delayed/default & $-8$/+10/+25 \\
FOIR & $<$20\%/20--30\%/30--40\%/40--50\%/$\geq$50\% & $-3$/+2/+5/+12/+20 \\
DTI & $<$1.5/1.5--3/3--5/$\geq$5 & $-2$/+4/+10/+18 \\
Bureau & per inquiry (cap 5) & +4 each \\
Insurance special & savings\_rate$\geq$0.30 & $-5$ \\
Writeoff & flag=1 & fixed score 95 \\
Default & flag=1 & fixed score 90 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\section{Recommendation Engine — 8 Steps}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|p{11.5cm}|}
\hline
\rowcolor{darkblue}\textcolor{white}{\textbf{Step}} & \textcolor{white}{\textbf{Rule}} \\
\hline
1 & \textbf{DTI Blocker}: DTI $\geq$ 5$\times$ blocks all new credit products \\
\hline
2 & \textbf{Writeoff/Default Blocker}: flags block all credit except insurance (+ gold loan for default) \\
\hline
3 & \textbf{Age Gate}: home loan age$\leq$55; car$\leq$62; education 17--35; business 23--65; consumer 21--60 \\
\hline
4 & \textbf{Occupation Gate}: students blocked from home/car/personal/business loans \\
\hline
5 & \textbf{Affordability Gate}: headroom = max(0, 0.50$-$FOIR)$\times$income must $\geq$ min\_EMI for product. Gold loan exempt (secured). \\
\hline
6 & \textbf{ML Score}: XGBoost+CatBoost ensemble gives probability per product \\
\hline
7 & \textbf{Bank Risk Score}: computed per product using formula in Section 4 \\
\hline
8 & \textbf{Utility + Ranking}: utility = (ML$\times$0.45 + profit$\times$0.35 $-$ risk$\times$0.20)$\times$saturation; ineligible = 0; sort descending \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{What is displayed:}

  \item Rank-2 recommendation card: same fields

  \item 9-product risk table: bank risk score + label for every product (shows what's safe vs risky for this customer)
  \item Customer profile sidebar: all raw attributes pulled from CSVs

\subsection{Tab 2 — AI Chatbot}

\textbf{Purpose:} Let relationship managers or customers ask natural-language questions about recommendations (e.g.\ ``Why was I recommended a personal loan and not a home loan?'').

\textbf{Implementation:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item LLM: \textbf{Llama 3.1 8B} via Groq API (fast inference, free tier)
  \item System prompt: injects the customer's full financial profile + validated recommendation + SHAP reasons + bank risk scores
  \item Guard rails: the prompt explicitly instructs the model not to fabricate SHAP values, not to mis-label FOIR or risk levels, and to decline questions outside the customer's profile
  \item Conversation state: stored in \texttt{st.session\_state} for multi-turn dialogue
  \item Context limit handling: if SHAP data is unavailable (customer not in test set), chatbot notes this and answers from profile only
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Tab 3 — New Customer Prediction}

\textbf{Purpose:} Real-time recommendation for a customer who is NOT in the CSV (e.g.\ a walk-in customer or a new account).

\textbf{Form inputs (30+ fields):}
Age, occupation, income, CIBIL score, city tier, tenure, repayment status, FOIR, DTI, savings rate, digital adoption, investment intent, online/travel spend ratios, bureau inquiries, writeoff flag, default flag, all 9 product ownership flags, CC utilisation \%, balance multiplier.

\textbf{Inference pipeline:}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \texttt{engineer\_features(inp, feature\_cols)} constructs all 160 features
  \item XGBoost predicts probability for each of 9 products
  \item CatBoost predicts probability for each of 9 products
  \item Ensemble: $0.55 \times P_\text{XGB} + 0.45 \times P_\text{CB}$
  \item Eligibility gates applied per product (CIBIL, age, FOIR, writeoff)
  \item Utility score: $(P_\text{ML}\times0.45 + \text{profit}\times0.35 - \text{risk}\times0.20)\times\text{saturation}$
  \item Rank-1 = max utility; Rank-2 = second-highest
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{What is displayed:} CLV score (with formula breakdown), CLV tier badge (normal/elite/super\_hni), rank-1 and rank-2 product cards with confidence, loan ranges, EMI, bank risk score, full 9-product utility table.

\subsubsection*{Key function: \texttt{engineer\_features()} — 160 features from 23 inputs}

This function is the bridge between user form inputs and the ML model. Since the model was trained on CSVs with 160 columns, every single feature must be reconstructed from scratch. The function handles:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item Income group derivation, CIBIL bucket lookup, segment classification
  \item CLV score computation: $\min(\text{tenure}/15,1)\times30 + \{25,10,0\}_\text{repay} + \min((\text{CIBIL}-550)/350,1)\times20 + \min(\text{products}/5,1)\times25$
  \item CC financials: limit = income $\times$ 5.0; spend = avg\_spend $\times$ card\_pct[digital\_score]
  \item Risk category ordinal: HIGH(0) if blocked/default/CIBIL$<$650; LOW(2) if CIBIL$\geq$750+regular+bureau$<$3; else MEDIUM(1)
  \item Dominant spend encoding: ecommerce(0) if online$\geq$12\%; travel(4) if travel$\geq$15\%; education(1) if inv$\geq$1; else ecommerce(0)
  \item All 18 interaction terms, 22 binary boundary flags, 16 ordinal/label encodings
  \item Safe defaults for 8 assumed features (settled\_flag=0, lifecycle\_ratio=0, etc.)
\end{itemize}

\section{Key Design Decisions (Point-by-Point)}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=3pt]
  \item \textbf{Utility over raw ML probability}: raw probability biases towards products seen more in training; utility corrects by adding bank profit and penalising risk.
  \item \textbf{Hard gates before soft scores}: no ML confidence can override a policy rule — keeps the system legally compliant.
  \item \textbf{Isotonic calibration}: reduces model overconfidence (raw ensemble tends to output 0.95+ for uncertain cases).
  \item \textbf{SMOTE only for minority classes}: prevents SMOTE from distorting dominant classes; applied per-class when count $<$ 500.
  \item \textbf{CLV as feature, not just display}: CLV score (0--100) feeds directly into XGBoost — the model learns that elite/super\_hni customers have different product needs.
  \item \textbf{SHAP pre-computed, not live}: running SHAP at inference would be slow; instead top-3 features per customer are saved to CSV during training and loaded by the chatbot.
  \item \textbf{Synthetic data with RBI distributions}: customers are fake but their statistical properties match real Indian banking data — CIBIL distribution, spend ratios, repayment rates all calibrated to RBI published figures.
\end{enumerate}
